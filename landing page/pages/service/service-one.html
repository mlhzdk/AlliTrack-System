IP Surveillance System Troubleshooting & Maintenance Guide

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Follow these steps to identify and resolve common CCTV issues.

1. Camera Not Powering On

  1. Step 1: Ensure the power cable is properly connected to the camera and power source.
    2. ⚡Tip: Use a cable tester if you suspect the power cable might be damaged.
  2. Step 2: Test the camera on another PoE port or outlet.
    3. ⚠️Warning: Do not force the cable into the port; this may damage the camera or switch.
  3. Step 3: Verify the PoE switch or injector is functioning properly.
    4. ⚡Tip: Check LED indicators on the PoE port to confirm power delivery.

2. Camera Offline

  1. Step 1: Ensure Ethernet cable is securely connected to both camera and switch.
    2. ⚡Tip: Replace the cable temporarily to rule out a faulty connection.
  2. Step 2: Confirm camera IP and subnet match network settings.
  3. Step 3: Update camera firmware to ensure compatibility with NVR.
    4. ⚡Tip: Backup current settings before updating firmware.
  4. Step 4: Restart camera and resync devices as needed.

3. Poor or Blurry Video Quality

  1. Step 1: Adjust resolution and bitrate in the camera or NVR settings.
  2. Step 2: Clean the camera lens and check for alignment.
    3. ⚡Tip: Use a microfiber cloth to prevent scratches on the lens.
  3. Step 3: Check network bandwidth usage; reduce traffic or prioritize CCTV traffic.

4. Remote Access Not Working

  1. Step 1: Verify router port forwarding and NAT settings.
  2. Step 2: Check firewalls and ISP restrictions that may block required ports.
  3. Step 3: Confirm login credentials and reset as needed.

5. NVR Not Recording Footage

  1. Step 1: Verify hard drive is detected and healthy.
    2. ⚡Tip: Run a diagnostic on the HDD if your NVR supports it.
  2. Step 2: Ensure recording schedule is enabled.
  3. Step 3: Check storage capacity; enable overwrite or upgrade if full.
    4. ⚠️Warning: Overwriting old footage will permanently delete previous recordings.
