IP PBX System (Yeastar) Troubleshooting & Maintenance Guide

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting

Follow these steps to identify and resolve common IP PBX issues.

1. No Dial Tone

  1. Step 1: Ensure all phones are properly connected to the PBX network.
    2. ⚡Tip: Check network cables and switches for loose or damaged connections.
  2. Step 2: Verify that the PBX system is powered on and running correctly.
  3. Step 3: Restart the PBX system if necessary to reset services.

2. Call Drops or Poor Call Quality

  1. Step 1: Check network bandwidth; prioritize voice traffic if needed.
  2. Step 2: Ensure all VoIP endpoints have the latest firmware updates.
    3. ⚡Tip: Use Quality of Service (QoS) settings on routers to prioritize voice traffic.
  3. Step 3: Verify SIP trunk configuration and registration status.
    4. ⚠️Warning: Incorrect SIP credentials can cause call failures.

3. Unable to Make External Calls

  1. Step 1: Verify the trunk settings and ensure the account is active with the provider.
  2. Step 2: Check outbound routes to ensure they are correctly configured.
  3. Step 3: Review call permissions for the extension attempting the call.

4. Voicemail Not Working

  1. Step 1: Confirm voicemail is enabled for the affected extension.
  2. Step 2: Check storage capacity for the voicemail system.
  3. Step 3: Restart voicemail services if messages are not being delivered.

5. Extensions Not Registering

  1. Step 1: Ensure the extension credentials match those in the PBX system.
  2. Step 2: Verify that the IP address of the endpoint is allowed in the system firewall.
    3. ⚡Tip: Restart the endpoint device to clear temporary registration issues.
  3. Step 3: Check network connectivity between the PBX and endpoints.

Still unable to resolve the issue?
